Indiana Jones and the Last Crusade Synopsis

By Bella Colosimo

A 1989 American action-adventure film directed by Steven Spielberg

In 1912, 13-year-old Henry "Indiana" Jones Jr., is riding a horse in Arches National Park in Utah with his Boy Scout troop. Will exploring caves, Indiana comes across grave robbers who have found a golden crucifix belonging to Francisco Vazquz de Coronado.

When the grave robbers aren't looking, Indiana steals the crucifix, because "It belongs in a musuem!". The grave robbers chase Indiana and he hops onto a circus train. They travel through various train cars filled with exotic animals.

After returning back to his house and his father, Indiana has aquired both his classic whip and a phobia of snakes. The sherrif shows up and makes him return the cross to the robbers, who turn it over to a strange man wearing a hat. The leader of the robbers admires Indiana's bravery and gives Indiana his hat.

Harrison Ford as Indiana Jones

Flash forward to 1938 and Indiana is fighting the same strange man wearing a hat on a boat. We find that he has gone to steal the same crucifix from 1912. He recovers the item and donates it to Marcus Brody's museum.

He has to go back to his job as a professor for a little while, but overwhelmed with students, he sneaks out his office window to avoid them. Once he gets outside, he meets up with two henchmen who request his attendance at their boss's house.

Indiana goes with the henchmen to Walter Donvoan's house where he learns his father, Henry Jones, Sr. was searching for the Holy Grail using a stone tablet and has vanished. Indiana decides to look for his father following clues from a notebook he recieved stating his dad was in Venice.

Sean Connery as Henry Jones, Sr.

Beneath the library where Henry was last seen, Jones and Henry's austrian collegue, Elsa, discover a tomb that houses a First Crusader. A top the coffin lies a complete inscription of the location of the Holy Grail.

When they try to leae, a secret society, whose mission is to protect the grail, sets the oil filled water aflame, preventing their escape. Indiana is able to find a way out through the city sewers. Indy and Elsa are then followed by the secret society membesr on speedboats before Indy learns from one of them that his father is prisoner in Germany.

Indy finds his father and helps him break out from the place where he is being held prisoner by Nazi's. They then trek to Berlin to recover Henry's journal. While Indiana is there, he comes face to face with Hitler, who signs the journal.

On Rotten Tomatoes, the film has an approval rating of 88%

Indy and Henry follow the clues to Jordan where they find that the Nazi's are also trying to find the Grail. There is a big fight with tanks and it ends with Indiana barely escaping a sharp fall off a cliff.

The group then goes to a temple carved in a cliff and get siezed by more Nazi's. Henry gets shot and Indiana is forced to find the grail to save Henry's life. Indy enters the dangerous pathway and avoids traps to find the chamber that holds the Grail.

The film won the Academy Award for Best Sound Effects Editing

Indiana succeeds in reaching the Grail chamber where he finds a knight brother is still living. He must choose the correct grail from many false ones.

The Nazi's however reach the grails before Indiana and Elsa chooses a cup to give to her boss, knowing it is the wrong choice. Her boss drinks it and ages all the way to death, turning into a skeleton. Indy then picks up the true grail, drinks from it, and proves it's the right grail.

Indy returns to the chamber with his dad and pours the water from the grail over Henry's wound, healing him. Then, as they try to leave, Elsa attempts to take the grail with her, which is not allowed, and she falls to her death. Indiana and Henry leave unscathed.
